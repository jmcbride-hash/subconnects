## SECTION 2 — The Problem

**Roofing labor is the most broken hiring market in construction.**

- Crews get found through Facebook groups, parking-lot conversations, and whisper networks.
- There's no shared standard for what "qualified" means.
- Workers' comp ghost policies are common and hard to detect.
- Manufacturer certifications go to contractors, not crews — leaving most of the working labor force without a portable credential.
- $200K mistakes are routine. Good crews stay invisible. Bad crews keep working.

The industry runs on trust that doesn't scale. We're building the trust layer that does.

## SECTION 6 — What "verified" actually means

We hate the word as much as you do — most platforms throw it around without backing it up. Here's what we verify, by hand, on every crew:

**Insurance.** Certificate of insurance uploaded, then we contact the carrier to confirm the policy is active and not a ghost policy.

**License.** State contractor or business license, cross-checked against the state registry.

**References.** Two or more contractor references, each one **called by our team**. We ask about quality, completion, and how the crew handled the last job. We document what they tell us.

**Currency.** Verifications expire — insurance renewals, license expiry. Crews who don't renew fall out of search until they do.

No automated reference forms. No self-attestation. No checked boxes that pass for verified. Every yes is a phone call.
